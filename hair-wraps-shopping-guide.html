Best hair wraps for sleek hair

Why would you use a hair wrap?

Did you know that your cotton pillowcases are surprisingly abrasive? They can act like sandpaper on your delicate hair, leading to friction that causes frizz, flyaways and tangles. Cotton is also the culprit behind your dry and brittle locks- since the material is porous, it can actually suck out moisture and absorb the precious oils that your hair needs to stay healthy and shiny.

But by adding a barrier between your hair and your pillowcase, you can prevent all the problems associated with pesky pillowcases. Enter, the silk or satin hair wrap.

Is it healthy to wrap your hair at night?

Yes, wrapping your hair will prevent breakage and other damage, leading to healthier and better-looking hair. Hair wraps will also prevent your hair from drying out or tangling overnight. Whether it’s a silk scarf or a satin bonnet, anything to keep your hair away from scratchy pillowcases will be a big bonus for the overall look and feel of your hair.

Does hair wrapping help your hair grow?

Hair wraps won’t magically add inches to your hair overnight. But they can prevent damage like breakage and split ends- two big no no’s if you’re on the quest for Rapunzel-esque lengths. If you’ve recently had a trim to remove split ends, now is a good time to incorporate a hair wrap into your routine. If you want to supercharge your hair growth process, consider adding ginseng supplements to your diet and using an intense hydrating hair mask.

Are hair wraps effective on different hair textures?

For people with textured or curly hair- particularly afro hair- hair wraps have played a huge role in fighting frizz and locking in moisture for many years. But that doesn’t mean it doesn’t work on other hair types as well. The science is the same whether your hair is coarse and curly or thin and fine. However, the more dry and brittle your hair is, the more you’ll notice a difference if you start using a hair wrap every night.

The history of hair wraps

It’s important to acknowledge the history and culture behind beauty trends, particularly ones that have been prevalent in non-white cultures for many years. Black women have been wrapping their hair for years as a way to prevent dehydration and keep curls defined and frizz-free. Now the beauty trend is becoming more commonplace among other cultures and ethnicities.

How do you wrap up your hair with a scarf?

If you have a satin or silk scarf, this can easily work as a hair wrap as long as it’s wide enough. All you need to do is comb your hair and gather it in a bun. Fold the scarf in half, then drape it over your head and tie two loose ends at the back below the bun.

You can then wrap the two loose ends around the bun, or you can pull down the top of the scarf that’s covering your head so that it covers the bun as well. You need to make sure the scarf is big enough, otherwise this won’t really work. If you find this method fiddly, consider purchasing an inexpensive, yet good quality good hair wrap or bonnet (scroll down for our pick of the best).

Silk hair wrap vs silk pillowcase

There’s an argument to be made about silk pillowcases being more effective than hair wraps. So which is best for you? Well if you have shorter hair and you usually sleep peacefully at night without much tossing and turning, then a pillowcase is probably your top contender. If you tend to move around and you have a lot of hair, then opt for a hair wrap.

Satin vs silk hair wraps: Which is best?

The main difference between silk and satin is that silk is naturally produced by silkworms, while satin is a manmade synthetic compound. Silk isn’t suitable for most vegans, and it’s generally more expensive as well; however London-based brand Silke is leading the charge with inexpensive, 100% silk hair wrapping products. If you want to use exclusively natural fibres, then silk is the best choice. If you want to plump for satin, be sure to check out the ratio of fillers to fabric.

A good compromise between the two is charmeuse, a type of satin that’s lighter and has a better ratio of float threads than satin. It’s also a little softer and a little duller than satin, so it’s much more comparable to silk.

6 reasons to use a hair wrap while you sleep

1. Maintains your hairstyle

Whether it’s a bouncy blowdry from the salon (or a salon-perfect effect from the comfort of your home) or carefully-created curls that you don’t want to spoil, a hair wrap will maintain your ‘do and stop frizz, kinks and tangles in their tracks.

2. Prevents damage

No more breakage means healthier, fuller-looking hair.

3. No more bedhead

If you’re one to toss and turn, all that friction can cause knots that leads to an inevitable bird’s nest look when you wake up. Cute when you’re 5, less cute when you’re an adult with only twenty minutes to get out the door in the morning.

4. Preserve your curls

Natural curls can be prone to frizz; a hair wrap can put a stop to that. They can also help artificially-created curls from dropping and losing their shape and definition.

5. Cut down on washes

A hair wrap will encourage your scalp’s oils to evenly distribute from root to tip, meaning less concentration of grease around the roots and therefore less need to reach for the dry shampoo. It’ll also keep hair away from your face, where products like moisturiser and toners, as well as your skin’s natural oils, can contribute to a greasy build-up.

6. No need to style as often

Step away from the comb; a hair wrap will lead to tangle-free tresses that don’t require as much styling or brushing.
