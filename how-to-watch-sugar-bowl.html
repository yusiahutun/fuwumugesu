It’s one of the biggest games in the college football season, and if you’re wondering how to watch the Sugar Bowl 2024 live and for free, we got you. The Sugar Bowl has a rich history, dating back to its first game in 1935. It has hosted numerous memorable matchups and has been a part of college football’s postseason for many decades.

This year, the second-ranked Washington Huskies vs. the third-ranked Texas Longhorns, representing the University of Washington and the University of Texas at Austin, respectively. Going into this season, Texas players declared the theme for 2023/24 would be the “revenge tour”. The tour started with Alabama in September and then featured wins over TCU, Oklahoma State and Texas Tech. One final stop remained: Washington, the fifth team to beat Texas in 2022.

The stakes are high going into the Sugar Bowl. “Obviously this is for a national championship, this rules it all, but that’s been the theme of this year is we obviously wanted revenge,” Texas edge rusher Barryn Sorrell told ESPN. “It’s for all the guys that were here during that 5-7 year. We took some very tough losses and we took some very tough criticism behind it as well, so taking those moments and using it as fuel to get these wins.”

Meanwhile, the Huskies are thus far enjoying an undefeated season. “I’m not gonna lie, the D-line is good, but at the same time, they haven’t played our O-line,” quarterback Michael Penix told ESPN in assessing their rivals. “Their whole defense, they do a great job, and they definitely play good ball, but we’re not playing the 49ers D-line or the Eagles D-line, so we’ll be good.” Without further ado, here’s how to watch the Sugar Bowl live for free.

How to watch the Sugar Bowl 2024 live for free

DirecTV Stream offers a five-day free trial, starts at $74.99 per month after the trial ends, and includes ESPN, ESPN2, ESPNews, and ESPNU. Fubo offers a seven-day free trial, starts at $74.99 per month after the trial ends, and includes ESPN.

Sling Orange and Hulu+ With Live TV don’t have free trials, however, they are cheaper per month than the other options. Sling Orange costs $20 for your first month, starts at $40 per month after the first month ends, and includes ESPN, ESPN2, and ESPN3.
