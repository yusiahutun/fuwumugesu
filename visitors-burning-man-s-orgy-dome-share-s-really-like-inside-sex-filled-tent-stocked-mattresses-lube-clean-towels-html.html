Visitors to Burning Man's 'Orgy Dome' share what its REALLY like inside the tent

October 01, 2024
blog

For festival-goers who fail to find romance among the art, all-night parties, heat and dust of Burning Man, there is always the option of the 'Orgy Dome'.

The 24-hour air-conditioned tent at the eight-day festival in Black Rock City, Nevada, encourages people of all sexual orientations - whether they are poly-amorous or monogamous - to take part, and is said to feature massage tables, mattresses with clean sheets, lubricants, condoms and sofas.

The dome's Sacramento, California-based organizers, And Then There's Only Love, claim it is a 'safe, consensual, sex-positive, inclusionary and exploratory environment where adult couples and moresomes can play and love.

An estimated 70,000 people are set to attend this year's Burning Man festival, which started on Sunday and runs until September 5.

Last year more than 9,000 people visited the Orgy Dome. Such was its popularity that some visitors said they have previously queued to get in.

One visitor described the dome on Reddit as a 'nice set up, clean towels and cushions, and moderate climate control'.  

Visitors are reportedly met by staff who ensure that they are entering the dome with a partner.

The Reddit user warned it is 'generally not a writhing pile of ten or 15 naked bodies as the name might suggest,' adding: 'Usually the dome is occupied by couples, with some threesomes and moresomes.'

The Orgy Dome fan said that largely 'people keep to themselves' but that partner swapping takes place sometimes.  

The festival-goer added: 'Usually there are some people watching on the edges, voyeurism and having an audience are definitely in play here, so know that going in.
