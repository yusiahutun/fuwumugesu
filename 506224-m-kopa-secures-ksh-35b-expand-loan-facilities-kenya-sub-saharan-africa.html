M-Kopa Secures KSh 35b to Expand Its Loan Facilities in Kenya, Sub-Saharan Africa

May 14, 2024
blog
  • M-Kopa secured KSh 35 billion in funding to finance its expansion drive in Kenya and across Sub-Saharan Africa
  • The Kenyan startup offering asset financing facilities said the funding from Standard Bank and Japanese-based trading house Sumitomo Corporation came in terms of equity and debt
  • M-Kopa Solar CEO and co-founder Jesse Moore said the fund will drive economic and digital gender equality

Kenya's asset financing startup M-Kopa has secured KSh 35 billion in funding for expansion across Sub-Saharan Africa.

The company received funds from Standard Bank and the Japanese-based trading house Sumitomo Corporation to offer credit facilities to customers.

M-Kopa Solar CEO and co-founder Jesse Moore said the funds came in terms of equity amounting to KSh 7.5 billion and debt of KSh 27.4 billion.

M-Kopa loan services in Kenya

Moore noted the funding will be significant in sealing existing economic and digital gender gaps.

"As we continue to scale, we remain committed to building a sustainable business and closing economic and digital gender gaps. We are delighted to have the support of new and existing investors who share our vision and mission,” said Moore, as quoted by Business Daily.

M-Kopa, launched in 2011, enables customers to access a range of assets, including smartphones, solar power systems and health insurance., TV sets and radios, among others, on credit.

M-Kopa operations in Africa

The startup operates in Kenya, Uganda, Nigeria and Ghana and has served over three million customers with credit worth KSh 137 billion.

M-Kopa grew its roots in the rural areas, offering 'Lipa mdogo mdogo' services to customers and reliable access to electricity.

The firm has received more than KSh 22 billion from investors in over 16 rounds since 2011.
