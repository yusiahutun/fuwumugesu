Who is Nathan Fillion?

Nathan Fillion is a Canadian-American actor. He was born on 27 March 1971 in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. He is 52 years old (as of 2023), and his zodiac sign is Aries. Nathan is the son of Cookie and Bob Fillion, both retired English teachers. He has an older brother, Jeff. His father is of French-Canadian descent, and his mother is of English, German, Finnish, and Norwegian ancestry.

According to IMDb, Fillion has 106 acting credits (as of November 2023). He first gained recognition for his work on One Life to Live in the contract role of Joey Buchanan, for which he was nominated in 1996 for a Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Younger Actor in a Drama Series. He also played a supporting role as Johnny Donnelly in the sitcom Two Guys and a Girl.

He is best known for his leading roles as Captain Malcolm "Mal" Reynolds on Firefly and its film continuation Serenity and Richard Castle on Castle. In 2018, Fillion starred as John Nolan on The Rookie and was an executive producer on the show and its spin-off series, The Rookie: Feds.
