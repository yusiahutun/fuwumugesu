Darts Q-School summed up as star with 59 average wins while other on 91 loses

September 17, 2024
blog

Darts Q-School is back and already it’s thrown up some bizarre results.

The time for darting hopefuls to win themselves a tour card and join the likes of Luke Littler, Luke Humphries and Michael van Gerwen on the Pro Tour has arrived and it seems that the pressure of achieving player's dreams may be too much for some already.

And it seems the luck of the draw couldn’t be better shown than the fate of Ultan McDyer and Charlie Martin after the two came away with very different results despite having over a 30 points difference in their average.

Irishman McDyer faced Samuel Palmer – who spent last year on the Youth Development Tour – in what was far from a game for either player to remember as McDyer secured a 5-2 win in the quarter-final despite averaging just 59.47, marginally better than Palmer’s 57.09.

While in another game, WDF UK and Ireland rank No.45 Peter Jacques averaged 90.89 as he defeated Charlie Martin 5-3, a cruel way for Martin to lose as he averaged 91.53 – virtually having an extra dart at treble 11 every turn over McDyer.

Who do you think will win a tour card? Let us know in the comments section.


Q-School began on Monday and players will have the chance to win or reclaim their tour cards until Sunday (January, 14). The UK competitors boast the likes of Ally Pally nine-darter legend ‘Big’ Willie Borland, and former world champions Richie Burnett and John Part. While 2012 world champs runner-up Andy Hamilton, World Cup of Darts winner John Henderson and former Grand Prix and UK Open champ Robert Thornton all battle it out.

Also vying to win back her tour card is Queen of the Palace Fallon Sherrock, who was knocked out of the World Championships 3-1 by Jermaine Wattimena last month.
