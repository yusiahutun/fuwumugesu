Kenyans were filled with grief waking up to the news that Priscah Mwaro, a charming and charitable lady, had passed on in her house in Nairobi.

Even sadly, news about her demise came just a day after she had celebrated her birthday on March 7.

Until her death, Mwaro, who was mourned by many Kenyans, was an administrator at KenGen and regularly shared work-related information on her social media accounts.

What made her famous to many Kenyans beyond her circle, however, was her relationship and marriage to Sports Cabinet Secretary Ababu Namwamba.

Mwaro and Namwamba were married, and between 2015 and 2018, the two love birds, blessed with three children, charmed their admirers with their irresistible public display of affection and loved-up photos.

Things fall apart between Priscah, Ababu Namwamba

Unfortunately, it seemed this was not meant to last as grapevine of the two having parted ways and being in a rocky marriage infiltrated the public spaces around 2018.

Then, Mwaro, came out to dispel the claims, reiterating their marriage was solid as a rock, even sharing their affectionate images on social media.

Just as things seemed to be cooling down between the couple, it became apparent that the two finally went separate ways over what was described as irreconcilable differences.

In a report by Citizen, Priscah's brother Patrick Mwaro had indicated in a past interview that there were efforts to reconcile the two, but these, too did not bear fruits.

It seemed like the centre could not hold, and things fell apart if Patrick's revelation was anything to go by.

Infidelity claims and reconciliation efforts

There were accusations of infidelity between the two, and the family of Mwaro, considering that they had become relatives courtesy of children born in the marriage, was determined to achieve a reconciliation.

"I told him he is part of our family because they have three children with Priscah thus the ties between families couldn't be ignored.

"It was upon him to choose whether he still wanted to live with Priscah or not because you can't force a man to live with a woman. If he has decide to part ways with her so be it. I pleaded with him but he was adamant," Patrick was quoted by Citizen.

It is still not clear what led to the death of Mwaro, but a comment by a friend who sought anonymity that she had "given up the fight", stirred speculation.
