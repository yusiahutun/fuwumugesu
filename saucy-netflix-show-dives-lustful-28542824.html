'Saucy' Netflix show dives into 'lustful' romance between Henry VIII and Anne Boleyn

July 23, 2024
blog

The saucy secrets of Henry VIII and Anne Boleyn are being laid bare in a lustful new royal drama about the Tudors.

Blood, Sex & Royalty is the latest three-part Netflix drama to delve into the sordid depths of the royals, with storylines ranging from teenage sex to incest and murder.

Blood, Sex & Royalty, available from Wednesday (November 23), is being billed as a modern take on the lives of King Henry VIII and the woman who helped to change the course of British history – his second of six wives, Anne Boleyn.

Dr Owen Emmerson, curator of Hever Castle, Anne’s childhood home in Kent, and a consultant on the show, said: “Anne was like Diana, Meghan and Wallis Simpson all rolled into one.

“She was an outsider and she shook things up. She was probably the first woman ever to say ‘No’ to Henry, which drove him wild," adding: "Unwilling to just be a mistress to the King, she wanted to be Queen.”

The series charts Henry and Anne’s seven-year courtship, which led to their marriage and to her ultimately being beheaded.

Former Emmerdale actor Max Parker plays King Henry, while former Coronation Street star Amy James-Kelly portrays Anne.

Netflix said: “In the 16th century, Anne Boleyn navigates treacherous sexual politics and rises to become Queen of England. She forges history as she reshapes King Henry VIII’s world view.”

Dr Emmerson said: "When she refused to sleep with him, fascination became obsession. He would send long steamy letters describing exactly what he wanted to do to her.”

Those letters, which were stolen at the time, are now kept at the Vatican Museum in Italy, and were used to prove he had another woman and that was why he wanted a divorce and to break from the Catholic Church.

Dr Emmerson added: “Court was a hotbed of contra­diction. Sex was strictly prohibited outside marriage and yet we know that key players at Henry’s court had mistresses and extra-marital sex, as did Henry."

Henry started having affairs when it became clear his first wife, Catherine of Aragon, could not provide him with a male heir.

Dr Emmerson explained: “Contraception was illegal so if Anne had engaged in intercourse with Henry, there was a risk she would have become pregnant.

“But there is plenty of evidence they did engage in some sort of sexual relationship during their courtship from the letters he wrote to her," adding: "They definitely got to first base – and probably way beyond!”

Anne was Queen for three years and in that time she had three pregnancies.

While she gave birth to the daughter who would become Elizabeth I, she suffered two miscarriages.

And when Anne failed to provide the King with a male heir, his love for her turned to hatred, and she was executed for treason and adultery.
