Pro rodeo cowboy, 27, killed on horse after being struck by lightning

June 13, 2024
blog

A 27-year-old professional rodeo cowboy has been killed after he was struck by lightning on horseback with a rope in his hand. 

Popular ranchman Terrel Vineyard, of Oshkosh, Nebraska, was fatally struck on June 21 while riding his main steed, Dose, who also died, the local sheriff's office has confirmed. 

A maven calf and team roper who qualified three times for the World Series of Team Roping in Las Vegas, Vineyard died during the freak incident at his home Garden County - just two months before his wedding party with his wife, Stacey. 

The couple, who met in 2020, married privately on February 10 this year, and their wider reception had been planned for August. 

Vineyard's heartbroken wife said he had been a 'father figure' to her three daughters - Maddie Aubree and Blayke - and recalled them all dancing in their living room days before he died.

'He had success in the arena, but he was most proud of the friendships he made,' his friends said on a fundraising page in his memory. 'To know Terrel was to love Terrel'

Mourners were encouraged by the rodeo community to attend his Tuesday memorial service at the Garden County Fairgrounds Arena in Lewellen on horseback.

Vineyard was buried surrounded by his 'cowboy and cowgirl friends on their horses' at the Antelope Valley Cemetery near Oshkosh, they said. 

Tributes poured in for the popular horseman on social media, including an emotional Facebook post by his wife Stacey. 

'We were perfect for one another,' she wrote. 

'You showed me love, you adored me and made me feel like the most beautiful woman. You believed in me and supported me. 

'You were always so proud of the girls and I. Our time was short, too short, I want to be so selfish about it but I am holding on to the amazing years we did have together.'

'God needed you before we could celebrate our marriage but our dance party in the living room a few weeks ago, with girls in their dresses, our boots,' she added. 

Stacey said her husband had seen her in the dress she was planning to wear for their wedding party, and one of his 'selfless' final acts had been to buy one step-daughter her favorite donuts as a birthday treat.

Terrel and Stacey Vineyard married privately in February and had been planning a wedding party with friends and family in August. His heartbroken wife said she had shown him the dress she was planning to wear for the party days before he died 

Vineyard introduced his three step-daughters to ranch life, taught them to ride and 'stepped in seamlessly as a father figure', while starting a cattle herd and renovating their family home, friends have said in a fundraiser 

Vineyard had been riding since he was a small child - he grew up on a Nebraska ranch and learned to ride a horse at the age of four, before learning to rope at the age of six 

Coming from a family of ranch cowboys, Vineyard had been riding since the age of four. 

He was born in the Buckhorn Springs Ranch east of Keystone Nebraska, before the family - his parents Shawn and Nancy, and older sister Tori - moved to Oshkosh in 1999. 

At just six years old, Vineyard learned to rope on a horse named Ornery, and spent his days riding pastures, doctoring calves, fencing, and haying, and his first horse was called Cowboy. 

Roping is a western sport where a horseback rider chases a cow, and uses a lasso attached to their saddle to catch it. 

As a schoolboy, Vineyard won AQHA Horse of the year three times with Cowboy and qualified for Junior High National Rodeo Finals in Gallup, New Mexico, twice in team roping.

He went to LCCC in Cheyenne, Wyoming, on a rodeo scholarship in the fall of 2014.

Vineyard became the protégé of several mentors, and a gofundme created to support his family said he was 'great at making friends anywhere he was'. 

'He maintained his first friendships and made a million more along the way,' the page says. 

'He had success in the arena, but he was most proud of the friendships he made. To know Terrel was to love Terrel.'

He moved back home to Oshkosh in 2017, working alongside his father on the family ranch, and qualified three times for the World Series of Team Roping in Las Vegas with his horse at the time, Shorty.

Some 73 people have so far contributed $5,770 to the family fundraiser in the wake of Vineyard's death.
