Brother and sister, aged 17 and 14, are orphaned after their mom, dad, and baby brothers are killed

June 13, 2024
blog

A brother and sister have been orphaned after their mother, father and two baby brothers were killed in a semi-truck accident on an Oklahoma highway. 

Aldo Fernandez, 36, his wife Marissa Sanchez, 35, and their two young sons Sebastian, four, and Calvin, one, died after the family's Honda Civic collided with a tractor trailer on January 20 at about 6.30pm. 

It is unclear which vehicle caused the collision that took place on I-35 in Love County- about 100 miles north of Dallas and Fort Worth, Texas near mile marker 24 - but the family was not wearing seatbelts. 

The couple's older children, Aldito, 17, and Yitzel, 14, were not in the car at the time of the crash, which is currently under investigation. 

Ali Dolatabadi, Aldo's brother-in-law, said the tragedy has 'left an unimaginable void' in their family and has gone on to create a GoFundMe page to cover funeral costs and go toward Aldito and Yitzel's future. 

The diver of the semi-truck has been identified as Muhumed Abi, 31, from Savage, Minnesota. Police reported that he did not sustain any injuries from the collision and he was not arrested.

According to a police report, the weather was clear on the day of the accident and the roadway was also dry.

The parents and their two young sons were all pronounced dead at the scene of the crash and found with 'massive injuries.' 

A funeral service was held for the four family members on Sunday in Marietta, Oklahoma where a large group of people were seen around each of their flower-covered caskets. 

A Facebook video posted by Aldo's cousin Homar Fajado showed a large procession of trucks that trailed behind each other on a highway in honor of the family. 

'A whole family wiped out in one day due to a fatal accident. You just never know when your souls are required in heaven,' Fajado said in the post. 

Aide Dolatabadi, Aldo's sister also posted about Sebastian and Calvin and said: 'Your little lives had a special way to make my heart happy.' 

The cause of the crash is still unknown and is currently being investigated. Pictured: Yitzel is seen kissing one of her baby brothers

A GoFundMe page that was made in honor of the family and their teenagers went on to raise $77,983 for funeral costs and to go toward Aldito and Yitzel's future. Pictured: Marisss and her husband Aldo 

The couple's four-year-old son Sebastian (left) who died with them in the tragic crash. His little brother Calvin, one, (left) also died in the collision

Marissa's cousin Lizette Aldama Barrientos posted a collage of pictures of the mother and her family on Facebook and said: 'We will never forget you, Aldo, and those precious babies Sebastian and Calvin.' 

'Your memories will remain with us forever.'  

The donation page that was set up for the family originally set out to reach a goal of $25,000, but has gone on to raise $77,983. 

'It truly means the world to the families involved, especially the surviving kids,' Ali said on the page. 

'This is a testament as to how much Aldo, Marisa, Sebastian, and Calvin touched and impacted their family, friends, and community.'
