    We can't say enough good things about a Crock Pot for fall weeknight dinners and winter comfort food.

    The low-and-slow method of cooking turns root veggies and meat into meltingly tender meals, with flavors that have had time to meld together into so much more than the sum of their parts.

    Plus, when it's cold out and getting dark early, the last thing we want to do when we get home is to fire up the oven and start cooking something. It's such a treat to be able to come home to an already-cooked meal, like a little gift from our past selves.

    Get yourself a new slow cooker if you need one, then add a couple of these slow cooker recipes to your weeknight rotation. You'll be very glad you did.
