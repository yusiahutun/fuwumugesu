Love Island murder as dancer stabbed to death by 'jealous' fella in row about show

July 14, 2024
blog

A "beautiful" young mum and dancer was stabbed to death by her "jealous" boyfriend when she went to break up with him after they argued over an episode of Love Island.

Maddie Durdant-Hollamby, 22, was repeatedly knifed in the chest by her "controlling" partner Ben Green, 41, after she went to his home to end their relationship, an inquest into her death heard.

Green flew into a rage and stabbed Maddie three times at the property before turning the blade on himself - leaving officers to discover their bodies in pools of blood.

The killing happened at Green's address in Kettering, Northamptonshire, on August 27 last year.

A senior coroner was reduced to tears as details of the tragic murder-suicide were relayed to an inquest at Northampton's Guildhall on yesterday (Thursday, July 7).

Talented marketing executive Maddie was said to have gone to dump marketing director Green face to face rather than over text as she thought it was the right thing to do.

But within 10 minutes of arriving at the house, she had been stabbed to death and one of the knife thrusts was so forceful the blade snapped off in the wound. Green then went downstairs and took his own life, and was found with a deep wound to his neck and other self inflicted wounds.

Friends of Maddie, of Wimblington, Cambridgeshire, said how Green was a "jealous person" who regularly checked Maddie's phone.

Pal Abbie Green said: "I thought he was quite controlling. But he made Maddie happy.

"They had once been arguing because she found a Love Island contestant attractive. He would check her phone lock screen for messages."

She told her friends she would be seeing him the next day (August 26) after work to break up with him, and messaged them to call her if they didn't hear anything in an hour.

Friend Abbie added: "I started to think they had made up. It was not like her not to respond. I tried calling Ben and sent a text. I did not want to cause an issue for Maddie. The next day, I tried calling again but no answer."

At 1.30pm that day, officers were called to the house to do a welfare check and discovered the bodies.

Mum Rachel Durdant-Hollamby said: "Maddie was incredibly generous and enjoyed nothing better than spoiling her family and friends.

"I feel as though our lives have been destroyed, ripped apart. I can't quite see a way forward. There is a huge hole in our lives, I want to be a family of four again. She was just perfect.

"I'm trying to keep it together for Fabian [her son]. I want the rest of his childhood to be as great as Maddie's was. I just miss her.

"There's a huge hole in our lives where Maddie should be. She was just perfect and loving. We want her back.

"We were best friends. She'll always be my best friend. My heart is broken."

Maddie began seeing Green at Christmas 2019 after they met at the marketing firm where her dad Steve Durdant-Hollamby worked.

While Green was waiting for his house to be built, he even lived with Maddie's family for five weeks, befriending her dad and going out on runs with him. Mum Rachel said she didn't notice anything amiss with the relationship.

Recording a verdict of unlawful killing, senior coroner Anne Pember said: "In my 27 years as coroner I think this is the saddest case I've ever heard.

"I can't imagine your pain at the loss of your daughter. I am truly sorry for your loss."

A separate inquest then took place into the death of Green in which the coroner concluded that he took his own life.

The hearing was told he was married for ten years and had three children before his marriage broke down in 2007.

He had an amicable relationship with his former wife who, when she heard about the deaths said: "He wouldn't hurt a fly."

His brother Thomas Green told the court: "Ben is not violent. I think he would have reached out before doing something like that. It's come as a big shock."
