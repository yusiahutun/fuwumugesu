Take the inside track to the heart of Paris

September 15, 2024
blog

I think I know Paris but what I’d like is someone who knows it better to show me around. So I was intrigued when Eurostar announced it was launching exclusive Interludes breaks.

The train company has been experimenting on how it can give a city break the personal touch – based on that buzzy concept of ‘curated travel’ where things are tailored to individual wishes and interests. Along with a friend, I was invited on the inaugural trip earlier this year.

First we were asked a few questions… well, mostly what sort of restaurants we like. Classic French rather than foamy, I said, and left Eurostar to sort everything else out.

Trendy: The River Seine flows through the Marais district of Paris, where you can take a Eurostar tailored tour 

So we arrived at St Pancras with just our train tickets and no idea of where we were staying or what we’d be doing.

Two chefs, one from London’s cult Honey & Co, provided a special meal as we zipped under the Channel.

It turns out that you can tell a lot from your restaurant choice. If it dictated the choice of our hotel, Eurostar got my taste spot-on. The Providence, near St Denis, exuded full-on Gallic charm; the film Amelie by way of Vogue.

The rooms are all deep colours and velvet sofas. Ours was on the small side but featured its own cocktail bar. Overlooking a square, as I threw open the door to our balcony, a jazz trumpeter was busking in the street below.

And then Paris was ours to explore. We’d been given two-day Metro passes and queue-buster tickets to top exhibitions. Paris spooled out before us, every sunlit boulevard, corner cafe, and exclusive boutique.

We had tickets to a Rodin exhibition and, on the way, we walked through the Tuileries and stopped for an aperitif. It couldn’t have been more Parisian if Catherine Deneuve had been behind the bar.

However, things became really insidery when we had dinner at the home of British chef Ed Delling-Williams and his shoe-designer wife Zoe Lee. They live near the Gare du Nord in the hip Barbes area. Ed is head chef at Le Grand Bain in chic Belleville and is wowing the French with his nose-to-tail, no-waste food.

The Providence hotel, near St Denis, exuded full-on Gallic charm; the film Amelie by way of Vogue

Their home is a loft and in the kitchen area Ed and an assistant produced a series of dishes, including abalone and salads, followed by a whole suckling pig.

We drank natural wines, where there’s as little chemical intervention as possible. Ed’s a big fan and told us where we could buy some.

He also suggested we try out the The Clown Bar, next to Oberkampf. ‘The clowns used to go before their shows and the food is fabulous.’

Zoe said the best chocolate mousse comes from the cafe (Royal Bar) next to her show shop in the trendy Marais.

With our inside information, we started making plans for the next day… with chocolate mousse high on the agenda.
