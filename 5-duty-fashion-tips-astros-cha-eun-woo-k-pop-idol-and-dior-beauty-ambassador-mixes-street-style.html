5 off-duty fashion tips from Astros Cha Eun-woo: the K-pop idol and Dior Beauty ambassador mixes

September 04, 2024
blog
Cha Eun-woo is one of Korea’s – and the world’s – most familiar faces today, best known for having ideal facial features and body proportions. (Bless his parents.) An actor and a singer with boy band Astro, he’s also unsurprisingly a sought-after endorser representing Korean and global brands. Recently, Cha was introduced as an ambassador of Dior Beauty’s serum campaign.

But Cha deserves credit beyond his pretty face. His tall stature gives him that advantage, sure, but he’s also one to dress to the nines. The 26-year-old has cultivated his own off-duty style, mixing Korean brands with luxury names and striking the right balance of bold fashion choices and sleek, tried-and-true outfits.

Below are a few style rules we’re learning from the South Korean star.

6 of Shu Qi’s biggest luxury brand endorsements over the years

1. Go colour crazy

A true style savant is one who isn’t averse to colour. Cha shows us how it’s done by wearing bold hues with neutrals and playing with textures.

In one look, he rocks an emerald green jacket with Chaumet jewels and black leather pants. In another, he commands quiet attention in a soft turquoise jumper with a matching saddle bag by Dior.

Of course, like every true Korean idol, Cha has a wardrobe full of edgy leather jackets – but he goes beyond basic black. Here, he dons a 70s hippie look with a brown leather jacket and a floral print top underneath, styled with ripped jeans. He’s also been spotted in a 90s-inspired turquoise blue leather jacket, worn with a casual tee and jeans, and messy-but-sexy hair.

2. Make waves in white

How to wear white the non-boring way? Cop key pieces in shades of white that have a lot of texture. Case in point: Cha’s knitted white cardigan.

3. Channel chic in checks

Cha is also a fan of classics, such as stripes and a good checked shirt, hoodie or coat. He wears them while performing as well as in fashion events and campaigns, like this one for Burberry.

4. Be discreet with designer labels

While Cha occasionally wears loud-and-proud designer looks, such as coordinated monogrammed Burberry outfits, he usually dresses them down. And he styles his more muted, casual outfits with bags by Louis Vuitton and Burberry.

One moment you might spot him nonchalantly wearing designer shirts by Burberry or Balenciaga, and the next you might see him glamming up in a suit with Chaumet jewels or a rad watch by Hublot or Bulgari.

5. Grey always looks good

A black suit is likewise a staple, but Cha wears a lot of his well-tailored looks in grey, too. It’s just as smart, sleek and sophisticated, and grey makes a good playground for unexpected details, such as this backwards tie he wore.
