Battered 31-year-old car driven by Idris Elba on crime drama Luther sells for THREE TIMES its value

September 12, 2024
blog

A battered old runaround car made famous by a troubled TV detective has sold at auction for more than three times its listed value.

The 31-year-old vehicle - missing wheel-trims and matching panels - from hit BBC crime drama Luther went under the hammer at a car auction over the weekend.

The TV detective's iconic Volvo 740 GL driven by actor Idris Elba was sold at Anglia Car Auction on Saturday.

The beaten-up Swedish saloon had five former owners and a book price of £1,200 but sold for £4,160 after a bidding frenzy.

A vintage vehicle fan won the battered car at auction in King's Lynn, Norfolk.

The auctioneers stated the motor had no reserve price and MOT until January 2023.

They added: 'The critically acclaimed BBC psychological crime drama Luther, first broadcast in 2010, helped turn Idris Elba into an internationally famous star for his portrayal of DCI John Luther.

'One of Luther's trademarks was his love of old Volvos; in the first series, he drove a 240, and returned to that model for the fifth series.

'However, the big Swedish bruiser that got the most onscreen time with Idris was the pale metallic blue 1990 740 GL he drove in series two, three and four.

'H654 WKH is the Luther Volvo, registered in August 1990.

'It's in the same condition as when it appeared on TV, including missing bits of body trim and wheel trims, plus a front wing in grey primer.

'Ordinarily, these might be regarded as negative points, but in the case of this 740 GL, they provide extra authenticity; this is the car as DCI Luther owned and drove it in the series.

'Inside, the car still has its prop two way communications radio fitted, as well as what we believe is a blue light atop the dashboard.
