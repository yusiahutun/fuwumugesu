What to Keep in Mind

Fit

"First and foremost, the fit is important for comfort," explains Giulia Galgani, assistant director of training at Intimissimi. Being extra mindful of the sizing guide and customer reviews helps ensure a well-fit pair of undies, and ultimately how comfortable you'll be throughout the day. A helpful tip Katie Sturino, the author of Body Talk, uses is her Roll Test. "The roll test is a series I post on my Instagram where I bend over and see if my underwear rolls. This is the key to finding a great pair of comfortable underwear because if it rolls when you simply bend over, you’ll know it’s not going to be supportive to wear every day. "

The leg hole size and amount of stretch in a pair of underwear are also key factors in determining a good fit. Go for pairs that stay in place, feel supportive, and don't pinch, ride up, or slip. Some panties even have elastics, extra stretch, and thicker waistbands to prevent digging, indentions, and underwear lines for added comfort.

Fabric

Galgani suggests looking to natural fibers, like cotton, for an easy pair that's low maintenance yet comfortable to wear thanks to how breathable it is. (She also likes silk, but given how difficult it can be to clean, we suggest saving that for your lingerie, rather than everyday pairs.) For something particularly lightweight and breathable, modal or Tencel, which you can find in pairs like the Bombas underwear. And if you'd prefer a pair that looks particularly cute, try out a lace pair (we love Hanky Panky) that will look as good as it feels.

Style

Similar to bra shopping, there are many different types of underwear styles to choose from — including thong, bikini, high-waisted, cheeky, brief, boxers, g-strings, and seamless. So, depending on your preferences or needs — say, you prefer something full coverage or want a style that's extra comfy for lounging — shopping by style is a great place to start. To give you an idea of what to consider, Galgani recommends "cotton thongs or briefs for lounging, seamless thong, and string thong styles for working out, and cheeky panties for a mix of comfort and sexiness."

Your Questions, Answered

What underwear fit is the most comfortable?

It depends on your preference. While some women can't stand how a thong feels — though, we think almost everyone will love the weightless feel of the Commando classic thong — others will wear only thongs. Briefs in a soft material, like lightweight cotton or modal, that molds to your body universally will feel good, especially if they fit properly (i.e. not too tight), and a cheeky fit will also give you a little less coverage than a brief — some are even closer to the fit of a thong — but remain just as well-fitting. We suggest thinking about your favorite pair of underwear now and leaning on that cut and style to guide the rest of your shopping.
