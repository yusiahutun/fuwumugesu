Bulletproof G Wagon: Inside KSh 30m Mercedes Uhuru Kenyatta Drove to Azimio Memorial

July 25, 2024
blog
  • Retired president Uhuru Kenyatta attended the Azimio La Umoja coalition memorial for lives lost during anti-government protests
  • Uhuru drove himself in his favourite vehicle, a black Mercedes Benz G Wagon modified for maximum protection
  • The SUV, which he often drives, features bulletproof glass, run-flat tyres and other impressive features

Uhuru Kenyatta has arrived in style at the interdenominational memorial and prayer service by the Azimio La Umoja coalition.

Uhuru Kenyatta's bulletproof Mercedes

The service was held on Friday, July 28, at the SKM Command Centre in Karen, Nairobi.

Footage captured at the function depicted the retired president alighting from a Mercedes Benz G Wagon and being welcomed by Minority Leader Opiyo Wandayi.

Uhuru’s vehicle is an armoured model, evident in the beefy, run-flat tyres and thick glass.

The base model of the vehicle is over KSh 15 million, but Uhuru’s is much more expensive.

The cost of turning the vehicle into a fortress on wheels is around KSh 15 million and could reach KSh 50 million.

The armouring process includes reinforcing the chassis to protect the undercarriage and installing bulletproof glass.

The vehicle’s frame, shock absorbers and even engine are modified to handle the added weight of armour.

An air filtration system can also be installed to protect from gas attacks.

In some instances, armoured vehicles feature smoke screens to reduce the visibility of pursuers.
