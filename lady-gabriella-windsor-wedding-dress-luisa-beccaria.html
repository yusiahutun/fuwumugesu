Lady Gabriella Windsor's wedding dress designer on creating a gown for a modern royal bride

October 04, 2024
blog

Lady Gabriella Windsor married Thomas Kingston at Windsor Castle today, making it the third royal wedding to take place at the historic location in less than a year. And while those famous steps may be the same, the three women walking up them – and the gowns that they had chosen to wear – could not have been more different.

Lady Gabriella, or Ella as she is known by her friends, opted for a blush pink, lace gown by Italian designer Luisa Beccaria. And, despite the fact that this afternoon the gown perfectly matched the regal scene, the design actually started out life in a much less dramatic fashion.

“At the very beginning, Ella wanted something very fluid and pure,” Beccaria tells us. “She had a lot of different ideas about what kind of gown she wanted and they were not bridal at all. She didn’t want any volume, pleats, ruches or anything like that.”

Chris Jackson//Getty Images

Throughout the design process, the simple dress gradually grew into the more elaborate gown that we saw today. This happened as more details about the wedding were confirmed, most notably, the location and the tiara.

“When we learned that she would be getting married at Windsor Castle, Ella wanted the dress to have something a bit more regal about it. And then, when it was decided that she was going to wear this tiara that had belonged to her grandmother, Princess Marina of Greece, we started looking at pictures of her wearing it and decided to make the dress a bit more dramatic. We left it fluid at the front and kept it the same on the body, but then added the big skirt detail at the back.”

Chris Jackson//Getty Images

Designing a dress that is going to be seen in such a vast, regal space of course meant that nothing too simple could be worn. This dress, however, as Beccaria states, is not actually particularly voluminous – and what stands out on those steps is the unique fabric and the striking blush tones, which was something Ella knew she wanted from the very beginning.

“Just as she was sure that she wanted something quite fitted to her figure, she was certain that the dress should be a blush colour, rather than a beige or an ivory. After some trial and error, we eventually settled on using cream lace and cream embroidery, but sitting it on top of a layer that is made of several different shades of blush, which shows through.”

Chris Jackson//Getty Images

Creating a gown fit for a royal bride is a unique task, but Beccaria insists that she didn’t really feel any added pressure at all, because this is truly a different occasion to the Windsor Castle royal wedding that took place almost exactly a year ago today.

“With Meghan’s dress, there was a very specific aim and it was more about her personality coming out. She was walking up that staircase on her own and then being joined halfway by the future King of England. It was such a strong statement so the dress couldn’t be poetic or romantic. This simple dress had a very specific psychological message.”

With Princess Eugenie too, there was a story to tell, albeit a completely different one to the narrative Meghan had woven with her choice.

“Eugenie’s dress had a different meaning – it was a Disney moment, it was romantic and dreamy, but it wasn’t making a point.”

ANDREW MATTHEWS//Getty Images

To compare Ella’s gown, wedding and life in general to the other two is quite startling, Beccaria explains, for she doesn’t have the same pressures or attention on her. She is free to live her life as she sees fit and, although this fairytale moment took place today, this is not her normal life.

“Ella has this aesthetic environment that she is embracing completely for her wedding, but it is like a movie, it is not part of her real life. She is a completely free person, she makes a living and she lives a normal life.”

And all this is precisely why Ella is the perfect embodiment of modern royalty, Beccaria explains.

“This woman represents to me the modern princess. She is free and determined, she works like a dog, she is down to earth, she is humble and she talks to everybody. And then at the same time, she is very well-informed and interesting, she is incredibly cultivated.”

Chris Jackson//Getty Images

The honour in making the gown comes from who Ella is, what royalty means, and translating that into one very modern royal wedding dress.

“For all those reasons and because of the fact that she is such a special girl, I was so happy to make this dress. It was a case of studying how to nod to the tradition because, being a royal wedding, you cannot forget that there is this history behind it, but in the same way to project this woman into the modern world and interpret her own unique type of beauty, spirituality and soul into the dress.”
