Eyeliner looks | Kohl, liquid and gel eyeliner inspiration

May 06, 2024
blog

You’ve likely already got a favourite eyeliner in your make-up kit – be it a sharp liquid pen, soft kohl or precision pencil. There’s a high chance you’ve honed a signature look, too: perhaps a feline flick to elongate the eye, or a smudged-out wash of definition.

While eyeliner in itself is of course nothing new, now is the time to experiment with your technique, creating something that sits a little outside of your comfort zone.

The rise of attention-seeking eyeliner has been fuelled by the A-list. Rihanna has favoured a slash of tomato-red pencil, while the cast of Euphoria have been showcasing a series of sublime looks on the red carpet, bringing the show's scene-stealing beauty fantasies out from the small screen. And at this year's Met Gala, where bigger is always better, Cara Delevingne wore a crystallised cat eye, while Lucy Boynton demonstrated a dazzling twist on the '60s trompe l'oeil lash.

Stephane Cardinale - Corbis

There’s more inspiration to be found on the runway. At Chanel's spring/summer 2022 Haute Couture show, make-up artist Lucia Pica created brow-skimming geometric shapes that chimed with the show's constructionist references.

At Chloé SS22, James Pecis’ strategically placed orange swipes were a jolt of energy against the palette of neutrals, while Givenchy’s Kawaii lines were enhanced by outsized strip lashes. And at Dior – a brand fast making lashings of liner its signature – Peter Philips turned the traditional cat-eye on its head, using flicks to frame both the upper and lower lash lines. It's a simple yet entirely fresh way to open up the eyes.

Chanel

Taking this trend into real life may require some scaling back, but the opportunities for experimentation are endless.

Claire Mulleady, national make-up artist for Givenchy Beauty, believes the trend for look-at-me liner to be a timely one. “As mask wearing has endured, people are continuing to experiment with ways to play up their eyes,” she says, adding that when crafted correctly, a bold liner look can also be supremely flattering.

“Dramatic and colourful eyeliner looks are a fun way to be creative with your make-up, but they also have the potential to lift and elongate the eye and make your eye colour pop. The great thing about this trend is that there are so many ways to wear it, taking the elements that appeal to you the most.”

So, where to start? Of course, the runways’ visible-from-the-back-row designs are not crafted with your weekend plans in mind, but extending your usual liner flick beyond the lash line, or switching out classic black for chic navy, could be just enough to make your entire look feel fresh.

Victor VIRGILE

“When creating a big liner look I would definitely recommend mapping out the lines or shapes with a light pencil first,” says Mulleady. “This way it’s easy to adjust and create symmetry, which is often the trickiest part. Extra-bold liner won’t be for everyone, but you can ease into the trend just by using a bright colour. Try Givenchy Khol Couture Waterproof Pencil in Cobalt or Turquoise and create a twist on a classic flicked liner, or use it on the waterline for a surprise shot of colour. Blues, greens and lilacs feel fresh and wearable and no longer belong in the 80s.”

If the idea appeals but the execution intimidates, all it takes is a little practice and a few tweaks to your technique. “ For those new to liner or with a shaky hand I always recommend a using a waterproof kohl pencil first,” says Mulleady. “Draw your line and before it sets use an angle brush to smooth it out, removing any bumps or wobbles.

For sharper, graphic eyeliner looks, Mulleady recommends a liquid liner with a brush-style pen nib, which will “allow you to draw a line with the thin tip and build up to your desired thickness with ease. If you love experimenting or have been inspired by the return of TV show Euphoria, Givenchy Phenomen’Eyes Liners come in a variety of bright metallic shades and are perfect for a fun and dramatic above-mask makeup look.”

Eyeliner looks to embrace now

Punk precision

Use rebellious black lines to add edge to a feminine wardrobe, or neon brights to reinvigorate neutral separates.

The new graphic

Experiment with brush strokes that flatter the contours of your eyes, letting the sharp angles bring structure to a relaxed outfit.

Bold colour

A fluro flash is easier to wear than you might expect – and can instantly energise a pared-back look.

Clean and crisp

White eyeliner looks clean and fresh, however you apply it. Try tracing your lower lash-line to create the illusion of bigger, brighter eyes.
