Do YOU have this rare 5 coin? It could be worth LOADS of money

March 13, 2024
blog

Queen Anne was the first monarch of Great Britain and a special commemorative coin was sold in presentation packs by the Royal Mint in 2014 for £13.

But just over 12,000 were minted – making it the rarest £5 coin ever.

And now experts at the Change Checker website expect it could sell more than £50 if one becomes available for sale.

They think it will be in much demand as Prince Phillip’s 90th birthday £5 coin in 2011 – which regularly goes for more than £50 on eBay, because of its rarity.

Yasmin Britton, of Change Checker, said: “Collectors who are lucky enough to have the 2014 Queen Anne £5 coin in their collection, need to make sure they keep hold of it.

“Demand for this coin is expected to increase dramatically and is likely to fetch in excess of £50 on online market places.”

Although legal tender, £5 coins are rarely seen in circulation and are intended as souvenirs.

Meanwhile, this is not the first rare coin to fetch huge money online.

Earlier this month, a 50p coin was selling for a staggering £3,500 on eBay.
