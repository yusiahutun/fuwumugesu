About the Nursing Council of Kenya

The Nursing Council of Kenya was established by the Nurses and Midwives Act Cap 257 of the Laws of Kenya. The corporate body’s mandate is to set standards in Kenya's nursing and midwifery education and practice.

NCK’s mission is to:

Regulate and enforce standards in nursing and midwifery education and practice that inspires public safety and confidence.

The body achieves its mission by building capacity for nurses and midwives via training, registration, and regulation of their conduct. NCK works with various national, regional, and international organisations to ascertain that they meet the objectives of a well-regulated nursing profession.

The functions of NCK include:

  • Create and elevate standards of all branches of the nursing profession.
  • Improve nursing standards and health care in the community.
  • Provide the necessary avenue for training and instructions for those seeking registration and enrolment (with the approval of the Cabinet Secretary)
  • Conduct examinations for people interested in registration under this Act ((with the approval of the Cabinet Secretary).
  • Prescribe badges worn by registered nurses.
  • Recommend the institution to be approved by the Cabinet Secretary.
  • Have regard to the conduct of people registered under this Act.
  • Have regard to the standards of nursing care.

When is the NCK exam registration?

Did you know you can apply for NCK exams online? Registration for the April 2024 NCK Licensing Examination is open to all interested candidates, and the deadline is March 11, 2024. On top of this, the deadline for the withdrawal from the exam is March 29, 2024.

According to the NCK circular, all eligible applicants must have their application submitted for the exam by their training institutions.

How does one register for the NCK exam?

Once your training institution submits your interest, you will receive a link to your email. Follow the following steps to complete your registration:

  • Log in to your registered email (the one that received the email) and click on the “Complete Exam Application” link to update your profile.
  • The action will direct you to the NCK portal.
  • Click on the “Exams” button to move to the next step.
  • Once done, make the required payment to complete the application.

The Nursing Council of Kenya exams consist of four individual papers and three papers for other cadres. Every paper concentrates on specific competencies expected of nurses and midwives in their professions. You can always check the Nursing Council of Kenya exams past papers to familiarise yourself with the exam format.

How many times can one do an NCK exam?

You can take NCK resit exams a maximum of four times. Before getting approval or being notified they’ve passed, they must satisfy the Board of Examiners for each paper. They are eligible for enrolment/registration and licensing only after getting the appropriate approval.
